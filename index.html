Recipe Details

Preperation time: Prep time is typically around 15-20 minutes (Washing, cutting, pounding, grinding ingredients and parboiling meat)

Cook time:

Cook time 30-40 minutes

Servings:

6 people (you can serve with eba fufu or semovita any swallow)

Difficulty:

It not hard to prepare but might be stressfull than other soups


Ingridients

  • 15 cups Sliced fresh waterleaves
  • 1/2 cup of ground crayfish
  • 3 cups Ground fresh Ukazi leaves
  • 3 cubes of knorr (sweetener)
  • 1 stockfish head (medium size)
  • 2 medium-sized dried or roasted fish
  • 1.5 cups of palm oil
  • 1 cup of Periwinkles (optional)
  • Snails(optional)
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 6 Red fresh pepper
  • 2 KG Assorted meat

Instructions

  1. Grind the ukazi leaves, it acts as the thickener for this soup.
  2. Season the meat with a half cup of sliced onions, salt, two seasoning cubes and other spice of choice
  3. Slice the water leaves and set aside in a sieve so excess water will drain away.
  4. Wash the stockfish, dried fish and add them to the cooking meat, let them cook till they are soft and the pot is almost dry.
  5. Add the palm oil, crayfish and stir all together. Add one seasoning cube, ground pepper, snails and salt to taste, allow cooking for 3-5 minutes.
  6. Add the water-leaves; allow to simmer for 6 minutes while you stir continuously.
  7. Add the ground ukazi leaves. Stir all together, cover the pot and allow another 5 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes.
  8. You are done with your delicious Afang soup
  9. Serve with Eba, fufu or any swallow of your choice

Tips

  • The Afang (Ukazi) leaves are tough, so they must be pounded or blended into a fine consistency (usually with a food processor and minimal water) to soften them before adding to the pot. Sellers at African markets often offer a grinding service.
  • Waterleaf releases a lot of water as it cooks. After slicing and washing, drain it thoroughly in a sieve and squeeze out as much excess water as possible to avoid a watery soup.
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, cow skin/pomo, tripe, etc.) and fish (stockfish, smoked fish, periwinkles, snails) add depth of flavor. Cook these proteins until tender on their own, using minimal water, and reserve the flavorful stock.
  • Wash periwinkles with lime juice or grape juice to remove all slime, and soak/clean dried fish in hot water to remove bones and dirt.
  • Afang soup is known for its rich, palm oil base. Ensure you use a good amount of palm oil to get the authentic taste and color, adding it before the vegetables.
  • he key to a vibrant, fresh-looking Afang soup is to cook the vegetables for only a short time. Add the waterleaf first, let it simmer for a few minutes, then add the pre-pounded Afang leaves and cook for just 2-5 minutes more before turning off the heat.
  • Some cooks suggest not covering the pot when initially cooking the waterleaf to let excess water evaporate and to help it retain its fresh color.
  • Continuously taste and adjust the seasoning (salt, pepper, crayfish powder) throughout the process to achieve a balanced flavor profile.
